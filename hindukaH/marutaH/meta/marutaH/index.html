Berserks

Now the presence of marut-like deities in the raudra class is a fairly ancient trait of the Indo-European system. Some have recognized that the original berserks [not the human warriors who emulated them] who accompanied Odinn, the cognate of rudra among the Germanic peoples, were a potentially cognates of the maruts. Even as Germanic paganism was being extinguished by the evil of the pretamata, Snorri Sturluson recorded in the Ynglinga saga that the berserks, strong as bears or wild bulls, slaughtered men, but neither fire nor steel harmed them [Footnote 4]. This simultaneous comparison to bears and wild bulls (hence Germanic b├дr= bear; serk=shirt) is interesting because it is also used specifically for the marut-s in RV [Footnote 5].
